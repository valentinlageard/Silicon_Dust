  • Entertainment and information.
  • Habitat construction.
  • Manufacturing and heavy industry.
  • Mining, refining and energy.
  • Shells and augmentations.
  • Transportation services and logistics.

How is their corpo-culture (choose 1 or 2) :

  • Based on an ancient or new religion.
  • Money is Honor, Debt is Trust.
  • Pragmatic and ruthless.
  • Secrets, Codes, Initiations and Masters.
  • They think they are gods.
  • Vicious and perverted.

Name your corporation.

The Board

The Board are your employers and superiors. They give you your resources and your status and in exchange, you enact their directives. You're relatively free in how you proceed at enacting their directives but you must give them results. The Board satisfaction is mechanically implemented by the Board Clock. The Board Clock has 6 tracks that can be filled or unfilled. The less the Board Clock is filled, the more satisfied with you the Board is, the more it's filled, the less they're satisfied with you and the more likely they may "fire" you. At character creation, no tracks of the Board Clock are filled. During play, tracks are filled or removed depending on the Enacting directives move.

When you fill the sixth and last track of the Board Clock, you're "fired". You loose all your Exec moves and you may be on the lookout for a special unit sent to terminate you. In this case you may :

  • Change to another archetype and be hunted by your old employers.
  • Try to impress your Board by giving them an extremely valuable asset in exchange for your old job.

When you already have no tracks filled on the Board Clock and the Enacting directives move tells you to remove a track, instead you gain a 4-cred bonus by tracks you should have removed.

Angel

Your angel is : - A pseudofork of yourself. - A pseudofork of someone you were close to (who are they and what happened to them ?). - An AI with a personality based on someone famous (who ?). - An AI with a personality tuned to your taste (how did you program them to be ?)

Shell

You have a bioshell with 4-aug.

Spend your aug to select your augmentations (see Gear).

Gear

  • A wearable worth 2-cred.
  • A personal weapon worth 4-cred.
  • A luxury apartment, house or module with everything included somewhere nice and secured.

Gigs

As an Exec, you have no gigs, you're fully supported by your Privileges and you gain bonuses when your Board is fully satisfied.

XP and Improvements

When you roll a highlighted characteristic or if a move tells you to, you win XP. When you have 5 XP, you may choose one of the following improvement :

  • Get a new move from your archetype.
  • Get a new move from your expertise.

After your fifth improvement, you may also choose :

  • Increase by 1 your core characteristic (max +2).
  • Get a new expertise.

Exec moves

At character creation, you have the Enacting directives and Privileges moves. You may take other Exec moves as improvements.

Enacting directives

As an executive for a hypercorp, you must enact the directives of the Board, your superiors in the corpo-hierarchy played by the MC.

  • When you succeed at enacting a directive, remove 2 tracks to the Board Clock.
  • When you fail to enact a directive, add 1 track to the Board Clock.
  • When you attract too much heat, add 1 track to the Board Clock.
  • When you ask for additional budget, add 1 track to Board Clock and they give it to you.
  • When you succeed at securing a valuable asset or position by your own - initiative, remove 1 track to the Board Clock.
  • When you failed at securing a valuable asset or position by your lack of initiative, add 1 track to the Board Clock.

The MC may give, change, pause and take directives at any moment during play depending on the fiction. When the MC gives you a new directive, they give you a budget from 3 to 6 cred to enact the directive. You're free to spend it however you like. If you engage another player, the Board pay them 3-cred but you may need to add extra cred from your budget.

Privileges

As an executive for a hypercorp, you're used to live in luxury. At the beginning of session, you do not have to spend cred for your lifestyle or for your resurrection insurance : you automatically have a luxe lifestyle and a 3-cred resurrection insurance (identical or custom similar value shell).

Big fish

When you Intimidate someone by telling them the name of your corporation, you may use +Memetics instead of +Kinesthesia and you can treat a Miss as a Partial or a Partial as a Success if you fill a track of the Board Clock. In the fiction, the MC says the repercussions. You may decide to tell the name of your corporation after rolling.

Blackmail [+Memetics]

When you use your hypercorp secret databases to get leverage on someone, roll +Memetics.

Success : You say what you have on them.

Partial : The MC says what you have on them.

Miss : They're clean, at least on the record.

Preferential prices

Your hypercorp gives you a wide array of preferential prices : everything costs -1-cred for you.

Transhuman ressources

When you want the services of a specialist or a team, your hypercorp gives you services for free.

Specialist : You can make any expertise move with a +3 bonus for free provided you asked for the specialist beforehand. You may pay 1-cred to have a +4 bonus.

Team : You have a gangÂ small 3-harm 1-armor at your disposal. For a bigger gang, better armed and armored or with specific minimal weaponry, it will cost more.

VIP [+Memetics]

When you want a meeting with someone, send them your details and roll +Memetics.

Success : They meet you.

Partial : They meet you but they may have taken additional precautions.

Miss : They meet you but it may be a trap.
